Off the Record Launches in Greater Seattle Area

You are legally entitled to contest your traffic tickets, but city and municipal governments make the process inconvenient and cumbersome. Off the Record lets you do it in under a minute.

Website: https://offtherecord.com
Email: team@offtherecord.com
iOS App: https://itunes.apple.com/app/off-record-fight-your-traffic/id1032930471

Off the Record (OTR) is an app that makes it easy, convenient and risk free for drivers to contest their traffic tickets. OTR just launched its pilot program in Seattle on September 23, and received its first Seattle area client half an hour after going live. Since then, there's been a steady stream of drivers opting to use OTR to fight their traffic tickets.

An average of 2,600 Washington drivers receive a traffic citation each day, netting the state over $140 million each year. Only 5% of drivers choose to contest their tickets, but OTR believes that number should be closer to 95%. Drivers who contest their tickets have a greater than 93% chance of beating them. With OTR, drivers can now contest their tickets risk free - if a ticket is found committed, OTR issues a 100% refund to their client.

Fighting a ticket with OTR is easy. Simply take a photo of the ticket and answer a few questions. OTR will match you with a lawyer using their SmartMatch algorithm, which matches a ticket with the local attorney most likely to get it dismissed. Once that's done, drivers can manage the entire process, from start to finish, within the app. Drivers will be kept informed with real time notifications from their attorney. No court date, no meeting with an attorney, no mailing in your own ticket.

OTR is only available as an iPhone app right now, but the web and Android version will be released soon.
